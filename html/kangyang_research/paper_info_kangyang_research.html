Non-contact physiological monitoring of newborn infants using RGB camera
Published Wed 06 September 2023
Non-contact vital signs assessment is a valuable approach for monitoring physiological parameters, such as heart rate, respiratory rate, and peripheral oxygen saturation, without the need for electrodes or sensors. In our research group, our primary objective is to leverage the latest artificial intelligence techniques to assess the vital signs of newborns. Furthermore, we aim to develop innovative algorithms that can effectively mitigate the impact of varying light conditions and darkness on the extraction of vital signs. This is crucial for achieving accurate and reliable measurements in different environments. Our ultimate goal is to create a unified model that can simultaneously monitor heart rate, respiratory rate, and peripheral oxygen saturation in newborns. By doing so, we aim to address the issue of understaffing in hospitals, as this model could provide continuous and automated vital signs monitoring without the need for constant manual intervention. By harnessing the power of artificial intelligence and developing sophisticated algorithms, we aspire to improve the quality of care for newborns by enabling efficient and non-invasive vital signs assessment. This could lead to timely interventions and better patient outcomes, while also alleviating the workload on healthcare professionals and ensuring comprehensive monitoring in hospital settings.
