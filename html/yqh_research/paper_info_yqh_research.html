A hybrid supervised fusion deep learning framework for microscope multi-focus images
Published Wed 06 September 2023
Given the escalating incidence of breast cancer, there is an increasing focus on early detection and the implementation of efficient and accurate prognostic treatment programs. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) has emerged as a valuable tool for breast cancer screening and diagnosis due to its high sensitivity, clarity, and ability to provide multi-sequence imaging. In recent years, artificial intelligence-based deep learning technology has rapidly advanced in the realm of breast MRI image processing and auxiliary diagnosis. By leveraging deep learning techniques, it becomes possible to train a highly automated model for processing and analyzing breast cancer MR images, thereby enhancing clinicians' efficiency and accuracy when utilizing this technology.

Breast MRI image analysis using deep learning remains a formidable task in the field. (1) Amide Proton Transfer weighted (APTw) imaging, an innovative MR imaging technology with promising application prospects. Nonetheless, the clinical implementation of APTw imaging is hindered by its limited spatial resolution and lengthy acquisition time. Addressing the efficient utilization of the unique attributes of APTw imaging sequences for lesion segmentation and reducing in the required number of offset frequencies for imaging are pressing issues that necessitate prompt solutions. (2) The multi-sequence MRI data exhibit a vast and intricate scale, with each sequence type presenting unique characteristics and information. Effectively extracting and integrating features from different MRI image sequences to enhance training efficiency is a primary challenge in current research on deep learning model training. Furthermore, due to high scanning costs, variations in acquisition time, and diverse MR image acquisition protocols across hospitals, incomplete MRI sequences are common. Generating reliable analysis results remains a significant research problem as well.
